| (2) normalization | A normalization constant is written without discussing a zero selected component. | Implemented in Stage 2: `conditional` is total and may be zero; `normalizedConditional x Ψ h` requires `h : conditional x Ψ ≠ 0`. `norm_normalizedConditional`, `normalizedConditional_ne_zero`, and `normalizedConditional_unique` state the corrected semantics. |

| (3) reciprocal normalization | The printed `1/N_i` form tacitly assumes each relative-state row is normalizable. | `reconstruction` is the unconditional identity `Ψ = Σ_i b_i ⊗ conditional b_i Ψ`. `normalized_reconstruction` branches on nonzeroness: the nonzero term is `b_i ⊗ (‖conditional‖ • normalizedConditional)`, corresponding to `1/N_i = ‖conditional‖`; a zero conditional contributes zero and is never divided by its norm. |
